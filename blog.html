Getting Started

June 27, 2025

I recently started an internship with the EMU Center for Digital Engagement, which connects interns with startups and helps them grow their professional experience. I was matched with Bedestrian, a small robotics company that develops robots for delivery tasks. Their current focus is on creating robots that can deliver medicine, food, and supplies across a hospital campus.

I was assigned a project to develop an Android application that uses a gamepad to support controlling, monitoring, and demonstrating the robot's capabilities over Wi-Fi or LTE. The app will be built using Flutter, a UI toolkit that enables developers to maintain one codebase for multiple platforms, including Android and iOS. I had never worked with Flutter before, and one of the first challenges I faced was not knowing where to begin. I felt overwhelmed by the number of tools, components, and unfamiliar termsâ€”it all felt new and very different from what I was used to. To tackle this, I broke the project down into small, manageable steps. This approach helped me stay focused and gave me a few easy wins, which built my confidence and gave me momentum as I began learning the Flutter framework.
